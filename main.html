About Me

    I am a PhD candidate at the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department, Auburn University. I completed my M.Sc. in Electrical Engineering at Auburn University in 2022. I received my B.Sc. degree in Electrical & Electronic Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology in 2017.

    My research interests include information freshness, wireless networks, semantic communications, and machine learning. My current research focuses on timely inference over communication networks. I have developed an information theoretic tool to interpret the importance of fresh features on timely inference which play crucial role in a wide range of applications such as autonomous automobile systems, medical monitoring, industrial control systems, and robotics systems. My research has brought novel telecommunication framework and advanced scheduling strategies.

    I am currently on job market. My thesis overview will be profiled in the December 2023 issue of the ACM SIGMETRICS Performance Evaluation Review (PER) newsletter.
