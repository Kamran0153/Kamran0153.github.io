About Me

    I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Elmore Family School of Electrical and Computer Engineering in Purdue University working jointly with Prof. Mung Chiang and Prof. Christopher G. Brinton on different areas of communication Networks. Previously, I pursued my PhD at the department of Electrical and Computer Engineering in Auburn University. I was fortunate to have been guided by Prof. Yin Sun as my advisor. My PhD work started a new research area called "Remote Inference" which is a crucial part of Next-G wireless networks. I developed a novel information-theoretic tool to evaluate the importance of information freshness on remote inference and designed low-complexity optimal scheduling algorithms. Our algorithms have the potential to reduce inference errors by 10,000 times. Here is my PhD thesis: Timely Inference over Networks. My research interests include communication networks, information theory, machine learning, reinforcemnet learning, multi-armed bandits, and information freshness.
